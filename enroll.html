Enrolling at Onondaga

Before you enroll, there are a few things you should consider that can effect the process.
Getting a degree?

If you plan on completing a degree program at Onondaga you will need to:

Apply for Admission

Just want to take a class?

If you are only taking one class and do not want to enroll in a degree program you just need to:

Register for Classes

Been awhile since you were here?

If you are coming back after taking a semester or more off you should:

Contact Student Central

Additional Enrollment Requirements

In addition to completing the admission application or registering for a class there are a few additional important steps you will need to complete.

Immunization Requirements

New York State Public Health Laws 2165 (measles, mumps and rubella) and 2167 (meningitis) require all students attending college to provide proof of immunity to measles, mumps and rubella and to be informed of the risks of meningococcal meningitis and given the option to be immunized or declining immunization. Students born prior to 1957 do not need to provide proof of immunity to measles, mumps and rubella. Full details regarding immunization requirements.

Financial Aid

All students, regardless of age, may be eligible to receive financial aid. To apply just complete the application process steps. Also don't forget about applying for scholarships, including the Renew Hope Scholarship, a opportunity for students over the age of 21 that can save up to 25% in tuition costs.

Certificate of Residence

Students who are residents of New York State for at least one year immediately preceding registration but not of Onondaga County need to submit a Certificate of Residence in order to get the discounted tuition rate.

 

Registration

Registration periods for all semesters are advertised on the website and will also be noted on the Academic Calendar. Students wishing to register for more than 5 credits must be cleared for immunizations. English, reading and math placement tests are administered to ensure placement in the appropriate courses and are required prior to registration in courses related to each of these areas. Students lacking college-level proficiency must register for the appropriate non-credit course. Exceptions apply to some students.

Advising

All degree seeking (matriculated) students are encouraged to meet with an advisor before registering for courses. Advisors will assist you in creating an educational plan appropriate for your degree and will help you select courses that you will need to stay or track for graduation and/or transfer.

Physical Education Waivers

Physical education courses are required for some degrees. A waiver from physical education requirements may be granted to a student who meets one of the following criteria:

  • 30 years of age or older at the time of first matriculation
  • A physician's statement, precluding participation
